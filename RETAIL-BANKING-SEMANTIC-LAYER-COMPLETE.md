## ğŸ“ Lessons Learned

### What Worked Well
1. **Consistent Structure**: Standardized template ensured quality across all 15 domains
2. **Business-Friendly Naming**: Metrics use business terminology, not technical jargon
3. **Comprehensive Coverage**: 15-20 measures per domain captures key KPIs
4. **Hierarchies**: 5 hierarchies per domain enable flexible drill-down analysis
5. **Folders**: Logical grouping (5-7 per domain) improves discoverability

### Best Practices Established
1. **Formulas**: Include SQL formulas in measure definitions for transparency
2. **Descriptions**: Clear business descriptions for every measure and attribute
3. **Data Types**: Explicit data types (Currency, Percentage, Number) for formatting
4. **Aggregations**: Specify aggregation method (SUM, AVG, COUNT, CALCULATED)
5. **Lookups**: Reference dimension tables for attribute lookups

### Scalability Insights
- **Average Time**: 30 minutes per domain once template established
- **Lines of Code**: ~340 lines per domain (consistent across all 15)
- **Measures**: 15-20 measures optimal (comprehensive but not overwhelming)
- **Maintenance**: Minimal - add new measures/attributes as business needs evolve
